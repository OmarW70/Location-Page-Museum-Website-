Discover the Museum's Location

Grand Egyptian Muesum

The Grand Egyptian Museum is located in Giza, Egypt, just 2 kilometers away from the Giza Pyramids. It lies on a prime site with a stunning view of the pyramids and easy access from Cairo city. The museum covers an area of nearly 500,000 square meters and is positioned to serve as a gateway between modern Cairo and the ancient world. The location was chosen carefully to enhance the visitor experience by providing a majestic backdrop of the Giza Plateau, ensuring that the historical atmosphere remains intact while offering modern facilities. In addition to the museum's architectural grandeur, the location offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore Egypt’s ancient history while surrounded by one of the world’s most iconic landmarks, the Pyramids of Giza. The museum's strategic location also provides easy access to the city’s public transportation, making it an ideal destination for tourists and locals alike.

Grand Egyptian Muesum Map

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) stands as a breathtaking bridge between Egypt's ancient past and its dynamic present. As the world's largest archaeological museum dedicated to a single civilization, this architectural masterpiece near the Giza Pyramids offers visitors an awe-inspiring immersion into 5,000 years of Pharaonic history. Upon entering the museum's vast atrium, visitors are immediately struck by the dramatic interplay of modern design and ancient wonder. The soaring glass façade frames an unforgettable view of the iconic pyramids, creating a powerful connection between the museum's treasures and their original context. The space is dominated by the monumental Grand Staircase, where dozens of royal statues stand as silent sentinels, guiding guests upward through the chronological layers of Egyptian history. The museum's collection is nothing short of spectacular, featuring over 100,000 artifacts that tell the complete story of ancient Egypt. The crown jewel is undoubtedly the complete Tutankhamun collection, where all 5,000 items from the boy king's tomb are displayed together for the first time.

About Metro

The Cairo Metro is the rapid transit system of Egypt's capital, Cairo. It opened in 1987 and is one of the busiest metro systems in Africa. The network consists of three lines, with ongoing plans for expansion. It serves millions of passengers daily, offering an affordable and efficient way to navigate Cairo's traffic congestion. The system is heavily utilized by locals and tourists, providing an essential transportation link across the city. The Cairo Metro is a vital urban transportation system that connects key areas of Cairo.

Location To Museum By Metro

Metro + Taxi Route :
Take Metro Line 2 , Board the metro and travel to Faisal Station. This station is among the closest to the GEM.​

From Faisal Station:
Exit the station and take a taxi to the Grand Egyptian Museum. The taxi ride should take approximately 15–20 minutes, depending on traffic. The combined cost for the metro and taxi is around $1–$3 USD.
